BEST Overview

The BEST (Boosting Engineering, Science, and Technology) Robotics competition is an engaging program designed to inspire middle and high school students through hands-on experience in engineering and technology. Each year, teams of students design, build, and program robots to compete in a themed challenge, emphasizing creativity, teamwork, and problem-solving skills. Participants learn essential engineering principles while working collaboratively to meet specific competition criteria, which include robot performance, documentation and presentation. With a focus on real-world applications, BEST Robotics equips students with valuable skills and ignites a passion for STEM fields, preparing them for future educational and career opportunities.

The BEST Robotics website can be found here.

The 2024 BEST Robotics competition, themed "LOW G," marks the 32nd year of this exciting challenge. This year's competition takes us to the moon, where teams are tasked with building a rover capable of mining and collecting moon core samples, collecting moon rocks, repairing lunar landing site equipment, and safely transporting an astronaut, all within the context of the Moon's low-gravity environment. This competition pushes participants to design innovative solutions for real-world space exploration challenges while fostering skills in engineering, programming, and teamwork.
