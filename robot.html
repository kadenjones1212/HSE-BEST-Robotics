The Robot

Overall Design


The moon rover is designed to be able to complete a wide variety of tasks, from collecting samples to completing maintenance on a solar panel, all in a timely manner. The main pieces of the rover include an innovative drive train, a multi-function arm, a variable grabber, and a dump system.


The Wheels


The wheels were inspired by construction equipment since traction is necessary. Bicycle innertube tires were wrapped around the wheels to allow the robot traction on the tarp. To solve the issue of turning, a spiked skid wheel was designed to allow the robot to turn while keeping position on the sloped surfaces.


The Grabber


The grabber utilizes a double-sided claw that could fit around a tennis ball while being large enough to grab the four-inch side of the habitat module. Additionally, multiple layers for the manipulator provided greater stability.


The Arm


The arm uses a swing bar arm, allowing three hundred and sixty degrees of motion. To increase speed, the team placed the arm on a rotating base, allowing three hundred and sixty degrees of motion. Additionally, the arm serves a holding spot for the astronaut


The Dump


The dump allows for easy and fast removal of collected materials, while also doubling as a storage space for habitat modules. The dump is placed on servos to allow it to quickly drop the contained items.
